About Typhoon Enteng

Typhoon Yagi or typhoon Enteng, also known as Severe Tropical Storm Enteng in the Philippines, was a devastating tropical cyclone that struck Southeast Asia in early September. It was the eleventh named storm of the season and the first Category 5 storm of the annual typhoon season. Yagi was one of the strongest to strike Northern Vietnam, Hainan, and parts of Southern China, during the meteorological autumn, and the strongest since Rammasun in 2014. The storm originated from a low-pressure area and reached its peak intensity on September 5. Its remnants later formed a depression six days later. The combination of Typhoon Yagi and the southwest monsoon led to heavy rain over Luzon, widespread flash floods, and widespread damage in various areas. The typhoon caused at least 924 deaths, 476 injuries, and 1,134 people went missing, making it the deadliest Pacific typhoon since Typhoon Haiyan.        -Elijah Castro
